Civil War Timeline

November 6, 1860

Abraham Lincoln gets elected

Lincoln is the first president who is a Republican and is in a party who opposes slavery.

January, 1861

Southern States secede

Mississippi, Florida, Alabama, Georgia, Louisiana, and Texas secede from the United States.

April 12, 1861

The Civil War begins

The Confedracy fires upon Fort Sumter, South Carolina.

Late May, 1861

Richmond becomes capitol of Confedracy

Richmond was the second largest and most industrialized city

August 6, 1861

Confiscation act of 1861

Permits court proceedings for confiscation of property, including slaved people, that were used to help the Confedracy.

February 16, 1862

Surrender of Fort Doleson, Tennessee. "Unconditional Surrender" Grant

This critical fort on the Cumberland River left the river in Union control. Ulysses S. Grant nicknamed: "Unconditional Surrender" Grant.

February 22, 1862

The Confedracy's President

Jefferson Davis becomes President of the Confedrate States of America.

June 6, 1862

Battle of Memphis, Tennesee

Mississippi gets in Union control except for the west of the Mississippi where the city of Vicksburg, the last Confedrate hold on the river, is.

September 17, 1862

Battle of Antietam, Maryland

The bloodiest single day of the Civil War. The battle ends General Lee's attempt of invading the North.

January 1, 1863

The Emancipation Proclamation

The Emancipation Proclamation freed all enslaved people in the rebelling states, allowed Blacks to join the army, and it made white Southerners mad. This was an important step to restoring the Union to how it was without slavery.

July 1-3, 1863

Battle of Gettysburg, Pennsylvania

The Battle of Gettysburg was the bloodiest battle in the Civil War. The outcome of this battle removed all of Lee's chances of invading the North.

July 13-14, 1863

Gettysburg Campaign ends

The Army of Northern Virginia crosses the Potomac River and the Gettysburg Campaign ends

July 30, 1863

General Order 252

Any Union prisoner killed in violation to the laws of war, a Confedrate prisoner would also be killed

November 19, 1863

Gettysburg Address

Dedication of the Soldier's National Cemetery at Gettysburg. Abraham Lincoln delivers the Gettysburg Address.

March 2, 1864

Grant appointed Lieutenant General

Grant takes control of all Union armies the next day.

June 8, 1864

Abraham Lincoln nominated again.

Lincoln gets nominated for a second term as president

November 8, 1864

Abraham Lincoln gets reelected

Lincoln gets reelected as president for a second time.

December 15-16, 1864

Battle of Nashville, Tennessee

The Confedrate army is defeated and the threat to Tennessee ends.

March 4, 1865

Abraham Lincoln starts his second term.

Lincoln starts his second term as president in Washington D.C.

May 10, 1865

The Confedrate's president gets captured

President Jefferson Davis gets captured near Irwinville, Georgia.

April 9 and June 2, 1865

The Civil War ends

Lee surrenders to Grant at Appottamox Court House in Virginia. The Civil War is over.
